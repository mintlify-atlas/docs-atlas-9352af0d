## Example 3: Deep Dive on Risks & Opportunities

### Scenario
Detailed risk assessment for a financial stock (JPM).

```bash
$ python main.py
📊 Welcome to FinFram Screener
Enter stock ticker (e.g., AAPL): JPM
```

### Initial Output

```markdown
# Stock Analysis: JPM (JPMorgan Chase)

## Overview
JPMorgan shows low volatility and stable performance, benefiting 
from higher interest rates.

## Key Metrics
- **Average Price**: $185.20
- **Current Price**: $192.50
- **Volatility**: $6.80 (Low)

## Forecast
Stable to positive outlook. Net interest income benefits from rate 
environment. Strong capital position.
```

### Asking About Specific Risks

```
⤷ Enter your prompt:
List the top 5 risks in order of severity
```

**Response:**
```markdown
# Top 5 Risks for JPM - Ranked by Severity

## 1. Interest Rate Policy Reversal (HIGH)
**Impact**: Net interest margin compression if Fed cuts rates
**Probability**: Moderate (50% chance of cuts in 2026)
**Mitigation**: Diversified revenue streams (investment banking, wealth)

## 2. Commercial Real Estate Exposure (HIGH)
**Impact**: $50B+ exposure to troubled CRE sector
**Probability**: High (office vacancies at record levels)
**Mitigation**: Strong reserves, but losses likely

## 3. Regulatory Changes (MEDIUM)
**Impact**: Higher capital requirements reducing ROE
**Probability**: Medium (Basel III endgame rules pending)
**Mitigation**: JPM has strongest capital position among peers

## 4. Economic Recession (MEDIUM)
**Impact**: Loan losses, reduced deal activity
**Probability**: Low-Medium (30% recession odds)
**Mitigation**: Diversification, fortress balance sheet

## 5. Cybersecurity Threats (LOW-MEDIUM)
**Impact**: Reputational damage, regulatory fines
**Probability**: Ongoing threat
**Mitigation**: Industry-leading security infrastructure
```

### Risk Mitigation Strategy

```
⤷ Enter your prompt:
How can I hedge the interest rate risk if I invest in JPM?
```

**Response:**
```markdown
# Hedging Interest Rate Risk in JPM Position

## Strategy 1: Pair Trade
- **Long**: JPM
- **Short**: Regional banks (more rate-sensitive)
- **Result**: Captures JPM's quality premium, reduces rate exposure

## Strategy 2: Options Strategy
- **Buy**: JPM shares
- **Sell**: Covered calls at +10% strike
- **Buy**: Protective puts at -10% strike (collar)
- **Result**: Defined risk range, income from calls

## Strategy 3: Diversification
- **Allocate**: 60% JPM, 40% rate-beneficiary sectors
- **Rate-beneficiary**: Utilities, REITs
- **Result**: Portfolio-level balance

## Strategy 4: Direct Rate Hedge
- **Instrument**: TLT (long-term treasury ETF)
- **Ratio**: 20-30% of JPM position value
- **Logic**: If rates fall (bad for JPM), bonds rally (offset loss)
```

<Tip>
**Best Practice**: When analyzing risks, ask for both severity ranking AND specific mitigation strategies to make informed decisions.
</Tip>

## Best Practices from Examples

### 1. Progressive Questioning
Start broad, then drill down:
```
1. Initial analysis (automatic)
2. "What are the main risks?"
3. "How severe is the commercial real estate risk specifically?"
4. "What metrics indicate CRE problems are materializing?"
```

### 2. Contextual Prompts
Provide your investment context:
```
"As a conservative investor with 5-year horizon, is this suitable?"
"I'm looking for growth stocks for my Roth IRA. How does this fit?"
"For a dividend-focused portfolio, what should I know?"
```

### 3. Comparative Analysis
Run multiple stocks and ask comparison questions:
```
"Earlier you analyzed TSLA. How does this compare in terms of volatility?"
"Is the risk/reward better here than in other EV stocks?"
```

### 4. Actionable Questions
Ask for specific guidance:
```
❌ "Tell me more about this stock"
✅ "At what price should I set a stop loss?"
✅ "What earnings metrics would trigger a sell signal?"
✅ "Should I buy now or wait for a pullback?"
```

### 5. Scenario Planning
Explore different outcomes:
```
"What happens to this stock if rates are cut 3 times this year?"
"How would a recession impact the forecast?"
"What if the merger doesn't go through?"
```

## Prompt Engineering Templates

### Risk Assessment
```
"What are the top [N] risks for [TICKER] in [timeframe]?"
"How likely is [specific risk] to materialize?"
"Rank risks by [severity/probability/impact on price]"
```

### Opportunity Identification
```
"What catalysts could drive [TICKER] higher in [timeframe]?"
"What's the upside potential if [specific event] happens?"
"Identify underappreciated opportunities in the analysis"
```

### Technical Analysis
```
"Based on the volatility of [X], what's a reasonable stop loss?"
"Is the current price above or below fair value?"
"What price levels represent support and resistance?"
```

### Strategic Planning
```
"Should I buy all at once or dollar-cost average?"
"What position size is appropriate given the volatility?"
"What exit strategy do you recommend?"
```

### Comparative
```
"How does this compare to [TICKER]?"
"Is this a better value than sector peers?"
"Compare the risk/reward to [broader market/sector ETF]"
```

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

<Warning>
**Don't ask vague questions**

❌ "What do you think?"
❌ "Tell me more"
❌ "Is this good?"

✅ "What's your price target for next quarter?"
✅ "Explain the significance of the high volatility"
✅ "Is this stock suitable for a conservative portfolio?"
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Don't ignore the initial analysis**

Read the full initial forecast before asking follow-ups. Your questions should build on the information provided, not ignore it.
</Warning>

<Warning>
**Don't ask multiple questions at once**

❌ "What are the risks, opportunities, and best entry price?"

✅ Ask one at a time for detailed, focused responses
</Warning>
